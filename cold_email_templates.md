# Cold Email Templates — ITAD / E-Waste / Recycling Outreach
Apex AI Consulting | Von Descieux
Updated: April 2026

Tone: Peer-to-peer. You're talking to founders and VPs who get pitched constantly. Short, specific, no buzzwords, no pressure. The goal is a reply, not a close.

---

## Template 1 — First Touch (with DataTech case study)

**Subject:** Built a 1,127-lead pipeline for an ITAD company in 30 days

---

Hey [First Name],

Built something recently for a SoCal ITAD company that I thought you'd find useful — fully automated outreach pipeline: source, verify, personalize, send, track. 1,127 qualified leads in 30 days, no new hires.

Happy to share the case study if it's relevant to where you are right now. Takes about 4 weeks to stand up for a new client.

Worth a 15-minute call?

— Von Descieux
Apex AI Consulting

---

**When to use:** First outreach to ITAD, e-waste, or IT recycling companies. Lead has not been contacted before. Works best when you can reference the recipient's company by name or vertical in the opening line.

## Template 2 — Follow-Up #1 (3 days, no reply)

**Subject:** Re: Built a 1,127-lead pipeline for an ITAD company in 30 days

---

Hey [First Name],

Just bumping this in case it got buried. Not trying to oversell — just figured the DataTech results were specific enough to be worth a look if you're working through the same sourcing problem.

No agenda beyond seeing if it's a fit.

— Von

---

**When to use:** 3 days after Template 1 with no reply. Same thread. Keep it minimal — you're reducing friction, not adding pressure. One sentence of context, one soft ask.

## Template 3 — Follow-Up #2 (7 days, last attempt)

**Subject:** Last note — ITAD outreach automation

---

[First Name] — last one from me.

If outbound pipeline isn't a priority right now, totally understood. If it comes up later, the case study is at ydescieux@msn.com.

We're onboarding 3 clients this month at $2K setup / $1.5K monthly if the timing ever lines up.

Good luck with Q2.

— Von

---

**When to use:** 7 days after Template 2, still no reply. This is the final touch in the cold sequence. Close the loop cleanly — leave the door open without pestering. The specific pricing detail does work here because it gives them a number to hold onto.

## Template 4 — Referral Ask (when they say "not right now")

**Subject:** Re: [previous thread subject]

---

Appreciate the honest response — that's the right call if the timing isn't there.

One quick ask: is there anyone else in your network running into the same sourcing problem? I'm building out a few client slots and a warm intro goes a long way.

Either way, good luck with the quarter.

— Von

---

**When to use:** When a prospect explicitly says they're not interested or timing is wrong. Don't pitch again — just ask for the referral. This recovers value from a dead lead and occasionally surfaces a better prospect. Keep it short, keep it gracious.

## Template 5 — LinkedIn Connection Request

**Subject:** [N/A — LinkedIn connection note, 300 character limit]

---

Von Descieux — AI automation for ITAD and B2B companies. Built a 1,127-lead pipeline for a SoCal ITAD firm in 30 days. Thought your work at [Company] was worth connecting on. No pitch — just expanding the network.

---

**When to use:** LinkedIn first touch when you don't have a verified email, or as a parallel track alongside cold email. Keep it under 300 characters for the connection request field. Reference their company specifically if possible — swap "[Company]" for the actual name. The "no pitch" signal matters here; it lowers the guard without being dishonest.
